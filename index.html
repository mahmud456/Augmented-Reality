What is Augmented Reality?

Augmented Reality (AR) overlays digital information onto the real world, enhancing our perception and interaction with the environment.

What is AR?

AR combines real-world views with computer-generated images, sounds, and other sensory inputs to create an enhanced interactive experience.

Benefits & Limitations

Understanding the pros and cons of AR in healthcare

Benefits

Improved Accuracy

Enhanced precision in medical procedures and diagnostics.

Better Education

Interactive learning experiences for medical students and professionals.

Cost-Effective Training

Reduce costs associated with traditional training methods.

Increased Accessibility

Remote access to expert guidance and training resources.

Limitations

High Initial Cost

Expensive hardware and software implementation.

Limited Accessibility

Not all healthcare facilities can afford AR technology.

Technical Issues

Hardware limitations and software glitches can disrupt procedures.

Training Required

Staff need proper training to use AR systems effectively.

How AR Differs from VR

Understanding the key differences

Augmented Reality (AR)

Enhances the real world with digital elements

  • Real environment
  • Digital overlays
  • Aware of surroundings
  • Mobile devices
  • Medical applications

Virtual Reality (VR)

Creates a completely digital environment

  • Virtual environment
  • Full immersion
  • Isolated from reality
  • Headset required
  • Gaming & simulation
